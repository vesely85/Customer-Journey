Reflections & Roadmap

What we’ve learned. Where we’re going.

Two views of the same partnership — the candid lessons from the growing pains, and the shared ambition for what the next chapter looks like across ops, HR, finance, and intelligence.

1
Mar 2024 · TLM Kickoff

Don’t replace WinTeam. Coexist with it.

PJS had WinTeam as their ERP/payroll system of record. The temptation for any vendor is to pitch “just move off it.” We chose the opposite path — build a custom Winteam payroll export, treat their hierarchies as sacred, and slot in alongside.

Respecting a customer’s existing system isn’t a constraint — it’s the permission slip for every future expansion.
2
Summer 2024 · Mobile & Validation

Quiet wins compound into loud trust.

Mobile break overrides, timecard validation scripts, IP-restricted web punches, position prompts — each one was an unglamorous, invisible fix. Stacked together, they became the reason PJS was willing to take the bigger swing on HR a year later.

The boring work earns the right to do the exciting work. Never skip the first.
3
July 2025 · The Bryan Alignment

Reframe “technical issues” as process discovery.

Bryan Noteboom flagged that every fix was surfacing another issue. Instead of defending the tech, we reframed: many of those “technical” items were actually configuration adjustments forced by process refinement. We committed to a comprehensive open-items list, timelines, and a clear go-live milestone.

When a customer is frustrated, change the frame before you change the code. Joint ownership of the problem beats assigning blame every time.
4
Nov 2025 · The Efficient Hire Question

Be honest about your partner map.

When Bryan asked if we had an integration with Efficient Hire, the commercial temptation was to hint that we did or could. We said plainly: our seamless integration was with Synergi, and we’d explore Efficient Hire only if they asked us to. That honesty made the Synergi commitment stickier.

Customers can smell a forced answer. Truth about what you don’t have today is the fastest way to get credit for what you do have.
5
Nov 2025 · WOTC Payroll Data Gap

Find the gap before the customer does.

When we started wiring Synergi’s WOTC calc into PJS Austin’s payroll feed, we discovered we didn’t natively carry exempt status or OT breakouts the way their calculation needed. Sage recorded a Loom explaining the gap, the audit risk of imperfect data, and gave PJS the choice to route via WinTeam instead. The credibility won us the next upsell.

Proactive disclosure of a data gap builds more trust than a silent workaround ever will.
6
Q1 2026 · Leadership Transition

When the org chart changes, get in the room.

Brian Noteboom departed. Molly (consultant/new CFO), Jeremy, and Kirk entered the evaluation. The instinct is to wait for the invite. The right move is to fly down, lead with Houston’s WOTC go-live as proof of forward motion, and earn relationships with the new leaders before the evaluation becomes a procurement event.

Relationships don’t transfer automatically with a title change. Show up physically. Show up early.
7
Ongoing · Pricing & Consistency

Price the entity like it’s the customer.

PJS Houston is a separate legal entity from PJS Texas, but culturally they’re one family. When Evelyn asked about HR pricing in March 2025, we explicitly honored the same PEPM we had extended to PJS Texas. Consistency across entities was the currency that mattered to them.

Fairness between a customer’s entities is a value judgment. Get it wrong once and the conversation becomes about pricing forever.
8
Throughout · The Weekly Cadence

Calendar beats urgency.

Thursdays at 10am for HR. Weekly meetings with Stephanie, Matt, and KC that never stop, even when nothing seems to need escalating. That cadence is the reason Sage and Melissa could confidently tell Ryan in April 2026 that nothing in the client’s signals suggested a replacement evaluation was underway.

A reliable weekly heartbeat with a customer is the cheapest insurance policy in enterprise SaaS.
